Understanding brain representations of motor control

Our behavioural & brain imaging (fMRI, MEG) experiments aim to identify the neural mechanisms that govern human movement (think, reaching for your coffee) and clarify how they are affected by healthy ageing.

For this, we regularly apply machine learning & big-data approaches.

Dedifferentation of motor cortex in healthy ageing

Using Multivariate Bayesian Decoding (MVB) with two lifespan fMRI datasets we refuted the hypothesis that Hemispheric Asymmetry Reduction in Older Adults (HAROLD) is compensatory (i.e. where the non-dominant hemisphere 'helps out' with computations normally performed by the dominant hemisphere in older age). These results support alternate views that the ageing phenomenon reflects interhemispheric inhibition.

How the Human Brain Grasps Tools

A grasping fMRI experiment tested which brain regions represented actions with 3D-printed kitchen tools (e.g. a knife) (video example). As covered by Nature, the results mark an important step toward uncovering which brain regions evolved to support tool-use, a defining feature of our species.
